CHAPTER 2: PERIODIC CLASSIFICATION OF ELEMENTS

Short Revision Notes | SSC Maharashtra Board | Standard 10 Science

WHY CLASSIFICATION?
Classification = Arranging elements in groups by similar properties. Makes study easier for 118 elements.
HISTORICAL TIMELINE
1800: 30 elements | 1817: Dobereiner's Triads | 1866: Newlands' Octaves | 1869: Mendeleev's Table | 1913: Modern Table | Now: 118 elements

2. NEWLANDS' LAW OF OCTAVES (1866)
Every 8th element = Similar to 1st (Like Music!)
Concept: Elements arranged by atomic mass. Properties repeat after every 8 elements (like Sa Re Ga Ma).
Example: Li(1st) and Na(8th) both soft reactive metals | H(1st) and F(8th) both reactive
‚úì Merits
  • First periodic pattern
  • Worked for light elements
‚úó Demerits
  • Valid only till Ca
  • No space for new elements
  • 2 elements in 1 box (Co,Ni)

  • Group 18: Noble gases (0 valence)

  • Most nonmetallic: F (top-right)
  • Isotopes: Same At.No. = Same place
